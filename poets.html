Poets Who Changed History

Edgar Allen Poe

  • Born on January 19, 1809, in Boston, Massachusetts
  • Poe's most popular works include "The Raven", "Annabel Lee", and "To Helen"
  • Poe pioneered detective fiction and was known for his dark and unsettling imagery

William Shakespeare

  • Born in April 1564, in Stratford-upon-Avon, England
  • His most well known pieces include "Romeo and Juliet", "Hamlet", "Macbeth", and "King Lear"
  • Shakespeare used lots of unique wordplay and mixed comedy with tragedy in his plays
  • He is arguably the most influential writer of all time

Emily Dickinson

  • Born on December 10, 1830, in Amherst, Massachusetts
  • Dickinson's most popular works include "Hope is the Thing with Feathers", "I heard a Fly buzz - when I died", and "There's a certain Slant of light"
  • Dickinson used unconventional punctuation and capitalization in her work
  • She was known for her unique style and use of themes such as death, spirit, and nature
